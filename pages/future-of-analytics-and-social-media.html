The Future: A World of Artificial Intelligence

Author:

Alyssa Carrizales, Founder of 10Ray

"Unlike in previous periods of technological change, this change will come quickly and effect all jobs from hourly workers to CEO."

The future is coming. Some would say the future is already here. In either case, artificial intelligence is at the center of this debate.

Artificial intelligence promises many things. It promises faster decision making, more accurate measurements, the elimination of tedious and intensive tasks, and, of course, the fuel behind most sci-fi movies, human-like robots.

Although we're not to the point where robots could walk and live among us (though this video shows that is a work in progress), artificial intelligence does exist today in the form of machine learning algorithms that are theorized to change the way we work and the solutions companies implement for major challenges. This means that artificial intelligence could be working alongside people in the jobs they do today to make them simpler or faster or potentially take over the job altogether. The latter theory is one of the reasons artificial intelligence has many people worried.

Unlike in previous periods of technological change that have affected jobs (e.g. the industrial revolution), this change will come quickly and effect all jobs from hourly workers to CEO.

Today, artificial intelligence is developed enough to cover 21% of a doctor's responsibilities, 15% of a financial planner's responsibilities, and 20% of a journalist's responsibilities (watch the Artificial Intelligence Race documentary here to learn more about these stats. Originally aired in Australia on ABC.) Companies from all over the globe are stepping up to continue to improve these statistics and provide new solutions to today's problems through artificial intelligence, and although there are many hesitant to improve A.I., the artificial intelligence movement is well underway.

In order for companies big and small to prepare for the changes, it's important to understand where artificial intelligence may impact your company. The A.I. solutions available today (from medical scheduling to social media advertising) may not solve your immediate problems or fit your budget but that day is coming. Although no role is excluded from being affected or improved by A.I., the more formulaic roles may have to evolve the fastest.

For the companies who want to prepare for the future, 10Ray is here - helping you set the foundation for Analytics and Social Media for whatever comes next.
